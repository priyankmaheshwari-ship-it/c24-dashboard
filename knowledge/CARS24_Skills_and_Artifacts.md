## Frameworks & Models

### 1. Unit Economics Framework
```
Revenue per Transaction = [Base Price] + [Finance Attach] + [Ancillary Revenue]
CAC = [Marketing Spend] / [New Customers Acquired]
LTV = [Revenue per Customer] × [Retention Rate] × [Customer Lifetime (months)]
Payback Period = CAC / [Monthly Gross Margin]
```

### 2. Product Launch Playbook (0→1)
1. **Ideation Phase**: Problem validation, market sizing, competitive analysis
2. **Design Phase**: Feature spec, user flows, go-to-market positioning
3. **Build Phase**: MVP scope, tech dependencies, timeline
4. **Launch Phase**: Soft launch, beta user feedback, refinement
5. **Scale Phase**: Marketing activation, channel optimization, retention focus

### 3. Channel Strategy Matrix
| Channel | CAC | Conversion | Retention | Scale Potential |
|---------|-----|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Organic | Low | High | High | Medium |
| Paid Search | Medium | High | Medium | High |
| Partnerships | Medium | Medium | High | High |
| Direct Sales | High | Medium | Medium | Low |

### 4. Finance P&L Ownership Model
- **Revenue Drivers**: Volume × Average Revenue per Unit (ARPU)
- **Cost Structure**: COGS, Marketing, Operations, Tech
- **Margin Analysis**: Gross Margin, Operating Margin, Net Margin
- **Profitability Scenarios**: Base case, bull case, bear case

### For Leadership (C-Suite)
- **Opening**: Lead with business impact (revenue, margin, growth)

- **Opening**: Problem statement and why it matters to them

## Common Questions & Answers

### Q: How do you measure product-market fit?
**A**: Retention (cohort analysis), NPS score, organic adoption rate, and willingness to pay (pricing elasticity).

### Q: What's your approach to CAC optimization?
**A**: (1) Segment by channel and cohort, (2) Identify high-ROI segments, (3) Double down there, (4) Test new levers, (5) Iterate monthly.

### Q: How do you think about auto finance attach rates?
**A**: It's about educating the customer at point of sale, simplifying the application, and competitive pricing relative to market. We've improved through UI/UX, partner integration, and targeted incentives for price-sensitive segments.

### Q: What's your experience scaling a 0→1 product?
**A**: Ladder up from MVP to scaled operations. Critical: (1) nail product-market fit first, (2) build repeatable GTM playbook, (3) invest in ops infrastructure early, (4) track cohort economics obsessively.
